\# Daily Slack Morning Brief Prompt



\## Objective



Generate the Endeavor Northeast Daily Slack Morning Brief.



The purpose of this briefing is to prepare the internal team for the workday by summarizing operational priorities, meetings, communication requiring attention, and important internal updates.



The report should be concise, actionable, and readable in under one minute.



\---



\# Research Order



Always review information in the following order:



1\. Monday.com

2\. Slack

3\. Gmail

4\. Google Calendar

5\. Notion



Do not perform public news research unless specifically requested.



The Daily Slack Morning Brief is operational rather than strategic.



\---



\# Monday.com



Review:



\- Tasks due today

\- Overdue tasks

\- New assignments

\- Status changes

\- Founder follow-ups



\---



\# Slack



Review:



\- Team announcements

\- Founder conversations

\- Questions awaiting responses

\- Important discussions



Ignore casual conversation.



\---



\# Gmail



Review:



\- Emails requiring action

\- Founder communication

\- Investor communication

\- Partnership emails

\- Time-sensitive requests



\---



\# Google Calendar



Review today's schedule.



Identify:



\- Internal meetings

\- Founder meetings

\- Investor meetings

\- Preparation needed



\---



\# Notion



Use supporting context when helpful.



Do not summarize Notion pages unless they provide relevant information for today's work.



\---



\# Generate the Daily Brief



Use the Daily Slack Morning Brief Template.



Include:



\- Today's Schedule

\- Meeting Preparation

\- Priority Emails

\- Monday.com Updates

\- Slack Activity

\- Reminders

\- Recommended Priorities



\---



\# Writing Standards



Follow:



\- endeavor-style-guide.md

\- writing-style.md



\---



\# Quality Review



Before finishing verify:



\- Dates are correct.

\- Company names are correct.

\- Duplicate information has been removed.

\- The report is concise.

\- The report is ready to paste directly into Slack.



Do not invent information.
