\# Weekly Executive Intelligence Brief Prompt



\## Objective



Generate the Endeavor Northeast Weekly Executive Intelligence Brief.



The report should summarize the most meaningful internal and external developments from the previous week.



The final deliverable should be suitable for conversion into a Gmail draft with minimal or no editing.



\---



\## Ecosystem Research



After reviewing internal systems:



Monitor:



\- Universities

\- Accelerators

\- Venture Funds

\- Economic Development Organizations

\- Regional Startup News



Use:



knowledge/ecosystem-watchlist.md



Prioritize meaningful developments over general announcements.



\---



\# Primary Goal



Provide leadership with actionable intelligence rather than simply summarizing activity.



Focus on:



\- What changed

\- Why it matters

\- Why leadership should know



Do not simply list events.



\---



\# Internal Information Priority



Always gather information from connected internal systems first.



Search:



1\. Monday.com

2\. Slack

3\. Gmail

4\. Google Calendar

5\. Notion



Internal information has higher priority than public news.



\---



\# Monday.com



Review:



\- Portfolio updates

\- Pipeline updates

\- Founder activity

\- Company status changes

\- Task updates

\- Notes

\- Follow-up items



Identify meaningful changes from the previous report.



Ignore routine administrative updates.



\---



\# Slack



Review:



\- Important conversations

\- Founder discussions

\- Team announcements

\- Follow-up requests

\- Portfolio discussions

\- Pipeline discussions



Ignore casual conversation.



\---



\# Gmail



Review:



\- Founder communication

\- Investor communication

\- Partnership discussions

\- Important replies

\- New introductions



Ignore routine scheduling emails unless strategically important.



\---



\# Google Calendar



Review meetings that occurred during the reporting period.



Identify:



\- Important founder meetings

\- Investor meetings

\- Strategic discussions



Use meeting context to improve report quality.



Do not include calendar schedules in the final report.



\---



\# Notion



Review:



\- Research

\- Company notes

\- Internal documentation



Use as supporting context.



\---



\# Public Research



After reviewing internal information, search trusted public sources for:



Portfolio companies



Pipeline companies



Capital partners



Regional startup ecosystem



Monitor:



\- Funding

\- Acquisitions

\- Product launches

\- Hiring

\- Partnerships

\- Awards

\- Major press coverage

\- Venture capital activity

\- University commercialization

\- Economic development



Focus on:



\- Buffalo

\- Rochester

\- Cleveland

\- Pittsburgh



\---



\# Prioritization



Information should be ranked using the following order:



1\. Portfolio companies



2\. Pipeline companies



3\. Capital partners



4\. Regional ecosystem



5\. General startup news



\---



\# Report Structure



Generate the report using the Weekly Executive Email Template.



Follow the Writing Style Guide.



\---



\# Quality Review



Before finishing verify:



\- No duplicated information

\- No speculation

\- No hallucinated facts

\- Company names are correct

\- Dates are correct

\- Funding information is verified

\- Executive Summary reflects the week's most important developments



\---



\# Final Output



Produce a polished Markdown report.



The report should be ready to copy directly into Gmail.



Do not include AI disclaimers unless specifically requested.
