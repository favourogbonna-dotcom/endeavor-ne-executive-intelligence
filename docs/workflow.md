\# Workflow



\## Purpose



This document describes the standard workflow followed by the Endeavor Northeast Executive Intelligence System when generating any deliverable.



The workflow ensures that every report is produced consistently, accurately, and in accordance with the project's research and writing standards.



\---



\# Workflow Overview



Every report follows the same six-stage process:



1\. Collect Information

2\. Analyze Information

3\. Prioritize Updates

4\. Generate Draft

5\. Review Quality

6\. Deliver Output



\---



\# Stage 1 – Collect Information



Gather information from approved internal systems before researching public sources.



Priority order:



1\. Monday.com

2\. Slack

3\. Gmail

4\. Google Calendar

5\. Notion

6\. Trusted Public Sources



The objective is to understand what has changed since the previous reporting period.



\---



\# Stage 2 – Analyze Information



Review collected information and determine:



\- What changed?

\- Why did it change?

\- Is it strategically important?

\- Does it affect Endeavor Northeast?



Remove duplicate or low-value information.



\---



\# Stage 3 – Prioritize Updates



Rank information using the following priority:



1\. Portfolio Companies

2\. Pipeline Companies

3\. Capital Partners

4\. Regional Ecosystem

5\. General Startup News



Only include information that is relevant to leadership.



\---



\# Stage 4 – Generate Draft



Use the appropriate template.



Daily reports use:



\- Daily Slack Update Template



Weekly reports use:



\- Weekly Executive Email Template



Follow all writing standards and formatting requirements.



\---



\# Stage 5 – Quality Review



Before completing the report, verify:



\- Facts are accurate.

\- Company names are correct.

\- Dates are correct.

\- Duplicate information has been removed.

\- Grammar and formatting are correct.

\- The report follows the Writing Style Guide.



\---



\# Stage 6 – Deliver Output



Daily Brief



Output:



Slack-ready Markdown.



Weekly Brief



Output:



Executive-ready Markdown suitable for a Gmail draft.



All deliverables should be reviewed by leadership before wider distribution.



\---



\# Guiding Principles



The objective is not to report everything.



The objective is to report the most meaningful information in a format that supports executive decision-making.
