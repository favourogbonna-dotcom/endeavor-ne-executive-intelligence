\# Connectors



\## Purpose



This document defines the role of each connected system used by the Endeavor Northeast Executive Intelligence System.



Each connector contributes different information to the reporting workflow.



\---



\# Monday.com



Purpose



Primary operational data source.



Monitor:



\- Portfolio companies

\- Pipeline companies

\- Company stages

\- Founder updates

\- Internal notes

\- Follow-up tasks

\- Status changes



Priority:



Highest



\---



\# Slack



Purpose



Capture team communication and operational updates.



Monitor:



\- Founder discussions

\- Portfolio conversations

\- Pipeline discussions

\- Team announcements

\- Requests for follow-up



Ignore casual conversation and unrelated discussions.



Priority:



High



\---



\# Gmail



Purpose



Capture external communication.



Monitor:



\- Founder emails

\- Investor communication

\- Partnership discussions

\- Strategic introductions

\- Important replies



Ignore routine scheduling unless strategically relevant.



Priority:



High



\---



\# Google Calendar



Purpose



Provide meeting context.



Monitor:



\- Founder meetings

\- Investor meetings

\- Internal strategy meetings

\- Partnership meetings



Use calendar events to improve context.



Do not report calendar events unless they resulted in meaningful developments.



Priority:



Medium



\---



\# Notion



Purpose



Provide institutional knowledge.



Monitor:



\- Research notes

\- Company summaries

\- Internal documentation

\- Strategic planning documents



Use as supporting context rather than a primary source.



Priority:



Medium



\---



\# Public Sources



Purpose



Monitor external startup ecosystem activity.



Track:



\- Startup funding

\- Venture capital

\- Product launches

\- Partnerships

\- Accelerators

\- Economic development

\- University commercialization

\- Major ecosystem announcements



Focus on:



\- Buffalo

\- Rochester

\- Cleveland

\- Pittsburgh



Priority:



After internal systems have been reviewed.



\---



\# Source Priority



Always use information in the following order:



1\. Monday.com

2\. Slack

3\. Gmail

4\. Google Calendar

5\. Notion

6\. Public Sources









\## Preferred External Sources



When performing public research, prioritize:



\- University entrepreneurship centers

\- Technology transfer offices

\- Accelerator organizations

\- Venture capital firms

\- Regional business journals

\- Government economic development agencies



Avoid low-value press releases and promotional content.
