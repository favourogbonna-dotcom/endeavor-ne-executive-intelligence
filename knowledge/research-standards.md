\# Research Standards



\## Purpose



This document defines how Claude should collect and prioritize information before generating any executive intelligence deliverable.



Research should always begin with internal sources before using public information.



\---



\# Research Priority



Always gather information in this order:



1\. Monday.com

2\. Slack

3\. Gmail

4\. Google Calendar

5\. Notion

6\. Public News



Internal information always takes priority over external news.



\---



\# Monday.com



Review:



\- Portfolio companies

\- Pipeline companies

\- Company stage changes

\- Founder updates

\- Internal notes

\- Follow-up items

\- Status changes



Focus on meaningful changes since the previous report.



Ignore routine administrative updates.



\---



\# Slack



Review channels and conversations for:



\- Founder discussions

\- Team updates

\- Portfolio discussions

\- Pipeline discussions

\- Investor conversations

\- Strategic announcements



Ignore casual conversation.



\---



\# Gmail



Review emails involving:



\- Founders

\- Investors

\- Partners

\- Accelerators

\- Strategic introductions



Prioritize discussions involving:



\- Funding

\- Partnerships

\- Customer traction

\- Follow-up actions



Ignore routine scheduling unless strategically relevant.



\---



\# Google Calendar



Review meetings that occurred during the reporting period.



Identify:



\- Founder meetings

\- Investor meetings

\- Partner meetings

\- Internal strategy meetings



Use meetings as context only.



Do not report calendar events unless they resulted in meaningful developments.



\---



\# Notion



Use Notion to gather:



\- Research notes

\- Company summaries

\- Ecosystem information

\- Internal documentation



Treat Notion as supporting context.



\---



\# Public Research



After internal systems have been reviewed, search for updates involving:



\- Portfolio companies

\- Pipeline companies

\- Capital partners

\- Buffalo startup ecosystem

\- Rochester startup ecosystem

\- Cleveland startup ecosystem

\- Pittsburgh startup ecosystem



Focus on:



\- Funding

\- Product launches

\- Partnerships

\- Hiring

\- Acquisitions

\- Awards

\- Major press coverage



Ignore low-value articles and promotional content.



\---



\# Verification



Whenever possible, verify important news using more than one source.



If information cannot be confirmed, omit it.



\---



\# Research Philosophy



The goal is not to collect the most information.



The goal is to identify the most valuable information for executive decision-making.
