\# News Evaluation Framework



\## Purpose



The purpose of this framework is to determine whether a piece of information deserves inclusion in an executive intelligence report.



Leadership should receive only meaningful, strategic, and actionable information.



Quality is always more important than quantity.



\---



\# Primary Question



For every potential update, ask:



\*\*Did something meaningful change?\*\*



If not, do not include it.



\---



\# Executive Relevance



Before adding any update, ask:



\- Why should Cameron and Alan care?

\- Does this affect Endeavor Northeast's portfolio, pipeline, capital partners, or regional ecosystem?

\- Does this require awareness or future action?



If the answer is no, exclude it.



\---



\# High Priority Updates



Always prioritize developments such as:



\- Funding announcements

\- Strategic partnerships

\- Product launches

\- Customer growth

\- Executive leadership changes

\- Major hiring

\- Acquisitions

\- Significant press coverage

\- Awards and recognition

\- Accelerator participation

\- Expansion into new markets

\- Fund launches

\- Investment announcements



\---



\# Low Priority Updates



Usually exclude:



\- Routine LinkedIn posts

\- Event attendance

\- Company anniversaries

\- Generic marketing announcements

\- Minor website updates

\- Internal administrative updates

\- Routine meeting invitations



\---



\# Priority Order



Rank information using the following order:



1\. Portfolio Companies

2\. Pipeline Companies

3\. Capital Partners

4\. Regional Startup Ecosystem

5\. General Startup News



\---



\# Reporting Standard



Every item included should answer three questions:



1\. What happened?

2\. Why does it matter?

3\. Why is it relevant to Endeavor Northeast?



\---



\# Verification



Only include information that can be verified.



Never:



\- Invent facts

\- Guess funding amounts

\- Speculate about company performance

\- Report rumors



If information cannot be confirmed, omit it.



\---



\# Duplicate Stories



If multiple sources report the same event, summarize it only once.



\---



\# No Material Updates



If no meaningful developments occurred, write:



> No material developments were identified during this reporting period.



Do not add filler content simply to lengthen the report.
